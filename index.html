  • 95% prob Second OMC fuel hike of ₹2–4/litre The ₹3.14 hike was calibrated to INR 89. At INR 95.91, OMCs are already underwater again. Expect a follow-on hike within 7–10 days, possibly broken into two smaller revisions for political optics.

UPSIDE RISK · WHAT MAKES IT WORSE

  • INR breaks 99/100. Triggers formal capital controls. LRS may be cut harder. Watch RBI commentary daily.
  • Hormuz tanker incident. Any actual attack on a tanker reprices Brent to $130–140 within 48 hours.
  • FPI panic. If 10Y crosses 7.6%, debt outflows accelerate. Self-reinforcing INR spiral.
  • Monsoon weak start. IMD has flagged El Niño risk. Weak June monsoon adds food inflation on top.

DOWNSIDE RISK · WHAT MAKES IT BETTER

  • US-Iran ceasefire holds. Brent could fall to $95–100 in days. INR could recover 2–3 rupees.
  • RBI surprise rate hold + hawk pivot. Defends INR more credibly than reserves alone.
  • SPR coordinated release. US/IEA strategic petroleum release would cool oil curve.
  • Strong dollar reverses. If DXY softens (Fed dovish), all EM currencies including INR benefit.

What citizens should hear, plainly: the price increases over the past week — fuel, milk, bread, gold duty — are not the peak. They are the floor of a new range. The INR at 95.91 has not yet been passed through. That pass-through arrives in your supermarket bill, electricity bill, and EMI calculation over the next 14–21 days.

What to do practically: if you have a flexible foreign purchase (electronics, travel, overseas tuition payment), pay this week — not next. If you have a SIP, do not pause it on impulse — INR-weakness benefits export earners (IT, pharma) in your portfolio. If you operate a small business with imported inputs, lock in supplier prices now. If you rely on diesel for operations, expect fare/freight revisions to lag your costs by 2–3 weeks — that gap is your margin loss.

The forecast in one sentence: by 5 June, expect petrol near ₹104, CPI around 7%, INR at 97–98, and at least one formal capital control on outbound remittance. The defensive posture is no longer holding. The state will move to the next playbook in the next 21 days.
